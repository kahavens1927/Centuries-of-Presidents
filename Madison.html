Important Dates

  • Born March 16, 1751 in Orange County, Virginia
  • Served four terms in the U.S. House of Representatives in 1789
  • Became secretary of state after the election of 1800
  • Died June 28, 1836

Bio

John Adams was the oldest of twelve children and his father was a slave owner. He elected as the govenor of Virginia
in 1779. Adams was famously known for working with Alexander Hamilton on the Federalist Papers and
was a influential member of the Louisiana Purchase. Was president during the war of 1812 and the burning of the White House by British Soldiers.
