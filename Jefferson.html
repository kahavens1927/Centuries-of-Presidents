Bio

Jefferson was born to a wealthy slaver and at one time during his life had owned over 600 enslaved people
as well as fathering a multitude of children from enslaved women. Beyond the Declaration of Independence,
Jefferson was elected twice for govenor of Virginia and in 1789, accepted an invitation to become the secretary of state
for George Washington. Through difference of opinions, he was apart of a political division and became
the leader of the Democratic-Republicans in 1796, where he was named president in the same year.
